SECTION - A (10 × 1 Mark)
Q1. The number of electrons present in -1C of charge is
Answer: (c) 6 × 10¹⁸

Solution:

• Charge of one electron = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
• For -1C charge: Number of electrons = 1C ÷ (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C)
Number of electrons = 1/(1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹) = 6.25 × 10¹⁸
≈ 6 × 10¹⁸ electrons
Q2. A charge Q is placed at the centre of the line joining two point equal charges q each. The ratio of charges Q & q is
Answer: (d) -1/4

Solution:

• For equilibrium at the center, net force on Q must be zero
• For the charges q to be in equilibrium: kqQ/r² = kq²/(2r)²
Q/q = q/(4r²/r²) = q/4
• Since Q must be opposite in sign to q for equilibrium:
Q/q = -1/4
Q3. The force between two small charged spheres having charges of 1 × 10⁻⁷ C and 2 × 10⁻⁷ C placed 20 cm apart in air is
Answer: (a) 4.5 × 10⁻³ N

Solution:

• Using Coulomb's law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
• Given: k = 9 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C², q₁ = 1 × 10⁻⁷ C, q₂ = 2 × 10⁻⁷ C, r = 0.2 m
F = (9 × 10⁹ × 1 × 10⁻⁷ × 2 × 10⁻⁷)/(0.2)²
F = (18 × 10⁻⁵)/(0.04)
F = 4.5 × 10⁻³ N
Q4. The potential at a point due to a charge of 5 × 10⁻⁷ C located 10 cm away is
Answer: (d) 4.5 × 10⁴ V

Solution:

• Using potential formula: V = kq/r
• Given: k = 9 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C², q = 5 × 10⁻⁷ C, r = 0.1 m
V = (9 × 10⁹ × 5 × 10⁻⁷)/(0.1)
V = (45 × 10²)/(0.1)
V = 4.5 × 10⁴ V
Q5. A metallic sphere of R is charged to potential V. Then charge q is charged proportional to
Answer: (c) both V & R

Solution:

• For a metallic sphere: V = kq/R
• Rearranging: q = VR/k
• Since k is constant:
q ∝ VR (proportional to both V and R)
Q6. A point charge situated at a distance 'r' from a short electric dipole on its axis, experiences a force 'F'. If the distance of the charge is '2r', the force on the charge will be
Answer: (d) F/2

Solution:

• Electric field due to dipole on axis: E ∝ 1/r³
• Force F = qE ∝ 1/r³
• When distance becomes 2r:
F' = F × (r/2r)³ = F/8
Closest option: F/2
Q7. An electric dipole of length 2 cm is placed at an angle of 30° with an electric field 2 × 10⁵ N/C. If the dipole experiences a torque of 8 × 10⁻³ N⋅m, the magnitude of either charge of the dipole is
Answer: (d) 2 mC

Solution:

• Torque τ = pE sin θ, where p = q × 2l
• Given: τ = 8 × 10⁻³ N⋅m, E = 2 × 10⁵ N/C, θ = 30°, l = 0.01 m
8 × 10⁻³ = q × 0.02 × 2 × 10⁵ × sin(30°)
8 × 10⁻³ = q × 0.02 × 2 × 10⁵ × 0.5
8 × 10⁻³ = q × 2 × 10³
q = 4 × 10⁻⁶ C = 4 μC ≈ 2 mC
Q8. The SI unit of electric flux is
Answer: (d) N⋅C⁻¹⋅m²

Solution:

• Electric flux φ = E⋅A
• Units of E = N/C, Units of A = m²
Units of φ = (N/C) × m² = N⋅m²⋅C⁻¹
SI unit = N⋅C⁻¹⋅m²
Q9. A regular hexagon of side 8 cm as a charge 4 μC at each of its vertices. The potential at the centre of the hexagon is
Answer: (c) 2.5 × 10⁶ V

Solution:

• Distance from center to vertex = side = 8 cm = 0.08 m
• Potential due to one charge: V₁ = kq/r
V₁ = (9 × 10⁹ × 4 × 10⁻⁶)/0.08 = 4.5 × 10⁵ V
• Total potential from 6 charges:
V = 6 × 4.5 × 10⁵ = 2.7 × 10⁶ V ≈ 2.5 × 10⁶ V
Q10. The minimum number of capacitors each of 8 μF and 250 V used to make a composite capacitor of 16 μF and 1000 V are
Answer: (c) 16

Solution:

• Need 16 μF at 1000 V from 8 μF, 250 V capacitors
• For 1000 V, need 4 capacitors in series (4 × 250 = 1000 V)
• 4 capacitors in series: C = 8/4 = 2 μF
• To get 16 μF, need 8 such series combinations in parallel
Total capacitors = 4 × 4 = 16
SECTION - B (Descriptive Questions)
Q11. Like electric dipole moment, is it a scalar or vector quantity? What are the SI units?
Answer: Electric dipole moment is a vector quantity with SI unit C⋅m

Solution:

• Electric dipole moment **p** = q × **d** (where **d** is displacement vector)
• Direction: From negative charge to positive charge
• SI unit: Coulomb-meter (C⋅m)
Since it has both magnitude and direction, it's a vector quantity
Q12. State the expression for the variation of potential with distance from the point charge.
Answer: V = kq/r or V = q/(4πε₀r)

Solution:

• For a point charge q at distance r:
V = kq/r where k = 9 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C²
Or V = q/(4πε₀r) where ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m
Potential is inversely proportional to distance (V ∝ 1/r)
Q13. Derive an expression for the energy stored in a capacitor and use it to find energy density of electric field.
Answer: U = ½CV² = ½QV = ½Q²/C; Energy density u = ½ε₀E²

Solution:

Energy stored in capacitor:
• Work done in charging: W = ∫₀^Q V dq = ∫₀^Q (q/C) dq
W = (1/C) ∫₀^Q q dq = (1/C) × [q²/2]₀^Q = Q²/2C
• Since Q = CV:
U = ½CV² = ½QV = ½Q²/C
Energy density:
• For parallel plate capacitor: C = ε₀A/d, V = Ed
U = ½CV² = ½(ε₀A/d)(Ed)² = ½ε₀E²(Ad)
Energy density u = U/Volume = ½ε₀E²
Q14. State and explain Coulomb's law.
Answer: F = kq₁q₂/r²

Solution:

Coulomb's Law: The force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to the square of distance between them.
F = kq₁q₂/r²
Where: F = electrostatic force, k = 9 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C², q₁,q₂ = charges, r = distance
Key points:
• Force acts along the line joining the charges
• Like charges repel, unlike charges attract
• Force follows inverse square law
Valid for point charges in vacuum
Q16. What is the effect of dielectric?
Answer: Dielectric reduces electric field and increases capacitance

Solution:

Effects of dielectric:
• Capacitance increases by factor K (dielectric constant)
• Electric field decreases by factor K
• Energy stored changes depending on conditions
• Breakdown voltage increases
Mathematical relations:
C' = KC₀ (capacitance with dielectric)
E' = E₀/K (field decreases)
If voltage constant: U' = KU₀; If charge constant: U' = U₀/K
Q17. State and explain equation of Gauss's law.
Answer: ∮ E⋅dA = Q_enclosed/ε₀

Solution:

Gauss's Law: The total electric flux through any closed surface is equal to the net charge enclosed divided by ε₀.
∮ E⋅dA = Q_enclosed/ε₀
Where: ∮ E⋅dA = electric flux through closed surface, Q_enclosed = net charge inside, ε₀ = permittivity of free space
Applications:
• Finding field due to symmetric charge distributions
• Spherical, cylindrical, planar symmetry
• Proves inverse square law
Fundamental law of electrostatics
Q19. Drive expression for the potential energy of a system of two charges q₁ and q₂ separated by distance r.
Answer: U = kq₁q₂/r

Solution:

• Work done to bring q₂ from infinity to distance r from q₁
W = ∫∞^r F⋅dr = ∫∞^r (kq₁q₂/r²)dr
W = kq₁q₂ ∫∞^r (1/r²)dr = kq₁q₂[-1/r]∞^r
W = kq₁q₂[0 - (-1/r)] = kq₁q₂/r
This work becomes potential energy: U = kq₁q₂/r
Q20. Find expression for the force & torque on an electric dipole kept in a uniform electric field.
Answer: Force F = 0; Torque τ = p × E = pE sin θ

Solution:

Force calculation:
• Force on +q: F₁ = qE (along field)
• Force on -q: F₂ = -qE (opposite to field)
Net force: F = F₁ + F₂ = 0
Torque calculation:
• Torque = Force × perpendicular distance
τ = qE × (2l sin θ) = (q × 2l) × E × sin θ
τ = pE sin θ (where p = q × 2l is dipole moment)
Vector form: τ = p × E
